14.8. Tangent Surface Limitations

The algorithm used within the arithmetic element may fail if the part surface and drive surface are nearly tangent and the cutter contact point is at or near the tangency point of the surfaces. Figure 14.46 illustrates this condition.

Figure 14.46. Tangent PS-DS Failure

The difficulty arises from two sources. First, the algorithm used to actually position the cutter with respect to the part and drive surfaces for each elementary cut vector treats the surfaces as instantaneous planes. Because of this, the limitation described in Section 14.7 is also applicable to the case of non-planes. Second, the algorithm for determining the direction of the next movement of the cutter involves taking a cross-product of the surface normals of the part and drive surface. As the surface normals become nearly parallel, the cross-product vector becomes undefined. This type of failure is characterized by erratic cutter motion.
