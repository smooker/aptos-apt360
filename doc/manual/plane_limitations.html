14.7. Limitations on Usage of Planes

In theory, any two planes which are not exactly parallel have a line of intersection, and consequently could be used simultaneously as PS and DS, PS and CS, or DS and CS in an APT motion statement. In practice, the numerical precision of calculations and the algorithms used in positioning the cutter impose limits on the degree of parallelism which the APT system will process without failure. The exact limits are a function of the cutter geometry, programmed relationship with surfaces (tool on or offset from surface), cutter location with respect to surfaces, and distance from the origin of coordinates.

In general, no difficulty is encountered using planes which are more than one degree from parallel. With planes more nearly parallel than one degree, an attempt to position the cutter properly, is likely to result in error 901 or 902.

This problem can normally be eliminated through the use of a pseudo check surface. For example, in Figure 14.45 the cutter is to be driven along P1 past P2 and then along P2. By specifying L1 as the intersection of P1 and P2 and using L1 (considered a vertical plane by the arithmetic element) as a pseudo check surface, the problem is eliminated (assume PS as the plane of the paper).
